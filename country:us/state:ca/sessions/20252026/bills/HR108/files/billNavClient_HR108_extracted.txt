Digest KeyBill TextWHEREAS, Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is an under-recognized public health crisis. It is the progressive, gradual loss of kidney function that results in a decreased ability of the kidneys to remove waste from the body; andWHEREAS, CKD affects more than one in seven United States adults, an estimated 35,500,000 people, and more than 4,400,000 adults in California are affected by CKD while nearly 4,000,000 adults remain unaware; andWHEREAS, As many as 9 out of 10 people with CKD do not know they have the disease because it usually has no symptoms until the late stages; andWHEREAS, African Americans, Hispanic Americans, and Native Americans are at high risk for developing kidney failure due to high rates of diabetes and high blood pressure in marginalized communities; andWHEREAS, Less than 13 percent of people with CKD risk factors are tested appropriately; andWHEREAS, More than 85,000 people have end-stage renal disease in California, and thousands of Californians are waiting for a lifesaving kidney transplant; andWHEREAS, The five-year survival rate for a patient with end-stage renal disease remains dismally low, with roughly 60 percent of patients dying within five years; andWHEREAS, Regular screening for, and early detection of, CKD allows opportunity for disease management that can slow the progression of kidney disease and help prevent kidney failure; andWHEREAS, The month of March is designated as National Kidney Month, and March 12 is recognized as World Kidney Day to raise awareness of kidney disease and the importance of prevention and early detection; now, therefore, be itResolved by the Assembly of the State of California, That the Assembly proclaims the month of March 2026 as Kidney Month in California; and be it furtherResolved, That the Chief Clerk of the Assembly transmit copies of this resolution to the author for appropriate distribution.
