[ Filed with Secretary of State May 20, 2025. ] LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTACR 57, Patterson. Lexington and Concord Day.This measure would recognize April 19, 2025, as Lexington and Concord Day.Digest Key Fiscal Committee: NO  Bill TextWHEREAS, On the evening of April 18, 1775, Paul Revere and others riders alerted the countryside that British soldiers would be leaving Boston to confiscate and destroy stored arms in Concord and to arrest patriot leaders Sam Adams and John Hancock in Lexington; andWHEREAS, Local volunteers, mostly farmers and tradesmen organized into community militias and distinguished by the term “Minutemen,” answered the alarm and proceeded to Lexington and Concord to await the King’s soldiers; andWHEREAS, At dawn on April 19, 1775, on the Lexington Green, over 700 British troops, known as “Redcoats,” approached the Green and ordered the 78 patriot volunteers gathered there to disperse; andWHEREAS, A shot was fired,no one knows by who,that signaled the start of the war for American independence and came to be known as “The Shot Heard Around the World”; andWHEREAS, British commanders lost control of their troops and, in the ensuing skirmish on Lexington Green, eight patriot volunteers were killed; andWHEREAS, The Redcoats continued their march to Concord, where about 400 American volunteers from local communities gathered throughout the morning; andWHEREAS, A battle ensued at the Old North Bridge and the Redcoats were forced to quickly retreat to the center of town; andWHEREAS, The Redcoats then began their march back to Boston under a constant stream of fire from the American volunteers; andWHEREAS, The Battles of Lexington and Concord marked the beginning of the American Revolution; andWHEREAS, April 19, 2025, marks the 250 year anniversary of the Battles of Lexington and Concord, the first battles in the American Revolution; now, therefore, be itResolved by the Assembly of the State of California, the Senate thereof concurring, That the Legislature of the State of California recognizes April 19, 2025, as Lexington and Concord Day; and be it furtherResolved, That the Chief Clerk of the Assembly transmit copies of this resolution to the author for appropriate distribution.
