[ Filed with Secretary of State June 09, 2026. ] LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTSCR 177, Weber Pierson. World No Tobacco Day.This measure would proclaim May 31, 2026, as World No Tobacco Day.Digest Key Fiscal Committee: NO  Bill TextWHEREAS, The Member States of the World Health Organization (WHO) created World No Tobacco Day in 1987 to draw global attention to the tobacco epidemic and the preventable death and disease it causes. World No Tobacco Day is celebrated around the world every year on May 31st; andWHEREAS, This yearly celebration informs the public on the dangers of all forms of tobacco use, the manipulative tactics the tobacco industry uses to attract and addict new users, including engagement to hook the next generation, and what people around the world can do to claim their right to health and healthy living and to protect future generations; andWHEREAS, The theme for World No Tobacco Day 2026 is “Unmasking the appeal – countering nicotine and tobacco addiction” to expose how the tobacco and nicotine industry continues to reinvent and repackage its products to hook a new generation, particularly children and adolescents; andWHEREAS, Tobacco use is the leading cause of preventable death and disease in the world; andWHEREAS, Tobacco use kills more than 490,000 Americans each year, causing nearly one in every five deaths in the United States; andWHEREAS, The tobacco industry has a long history of targeting youth with flavored tobacco products through aggressive marketing and kid-friendly flavors; andWHEREAS, Tobacco industry marketing campaigns that purport harm-reduction strategies are designed to maintain addiction in current customers, attract new customers, and expand addiction amongst youth; andWHEREAS, The tobacco industry’s misleading tactic of marketing “next generation” products such as e-cigarettes, nicotine pouches, and heated tobacco products, as safe or alternatives to quitting follows a similar tactic used in the past of falsely marketing “light” and “low-tar” cigarettes as safer alternatives, despite lack of evidence supporting those claims; andWHEREAS, Nicotine is a highly addictive drug and can harm adolescent brain development, particularly the parts of the brain responsible for attention, memory, and learning; andWHEREAS, Nicotine products like pouches and e-cigarettes can contain massive doses of nicotine, and some e-cigarettes can contain more nicotine than 20 packs of cigarettes; andWHEREAS, Rural populations in California experience higher rates of tobacco use and related health disparities compared with urban areas, with Californians living in rural areas being 33 percent more likely to use tobacco products than those living in urban areas; andWHEREAS, Of all racial and ethnic groups in the United States, Black Americans have some of the highest rates of tobacco-related cancer, with more than 72,000 Black Americans diagnosed with a tobacco-related cancer each year; andWHEREAS, LGBTQ+ individuals experience higher rates of tobacco use—28 percent of gay or lesbian adults report any tobacco product use compared to 18.4 percent of heterosexual adults; andWHEREAS, Research shows that nicotine is one of the hardest substance-use dependencies to break; andWHEREAS, Evidence-based tobacco cessation services significantly increase the likelihood of successfully quitting and can reduce health care costs and improve health outcomes; andWHEREAS, The federal Center for Disease Control and Prevention’s Office on Smoking and Health, which has led the nation’s efforts to protect kids from tobacco addiction, helped tobacco users quit, and reduced tobacco’s devastating toll, has been eliminated; andWHEREAS, In March 2026, the United States Food and Drug Administration proposed guidelines lowering the scientific standard required of industry for certain flavored e-cigarettes, making it easier for flavored products to gain FDA authorization; andWHEREAS, In 2020, with strong bipartisan support, California enacted Senate Bill 793 of the 2019–20 Regular Session—a nation-leading, landmark law to ban the sale of flavored tobacco products; andWHEREAS, California voters overwhelmingly upheld Senate Bill 793 when subjected to a tobacco industry-funded referendum, with nearly two-thirds of Californians supporting Proposition 31 at the November 8, 2022 statewide general election; andWHEREAS, World No Tobacco Day is a day that unites governments, health organizations, civil society, and youth voices under a shared mission: to end the tobacco epidemic and secure a tobacco- and nicotine-free future for the next generation; now, therefore, be itResolved by the Senate of the State of California, the Assembly thereof concurring, That the Legislature declares that May 31, 2026, is “World No Tobacco Day” and encourages the private sector and state and federal governments to promote policies and programs that seek to reduce tobacco and nicotine use among all Californians; and be it furtherResolved, That California is committed to protecting our children from a life of addiction and illness by protecting the integrity of our nation-leading flavored tobacco ban; and be it furtherResolved, That the Secretary of the Senate transmit copies of this resolution to the author for appropriate distribution.
