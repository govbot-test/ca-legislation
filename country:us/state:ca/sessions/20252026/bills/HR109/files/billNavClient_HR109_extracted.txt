LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTHR 109, as introduced, Schiavo. Digest KeyBill TextWHEREAS, Janitors and other property service workers serve as the first line of defense against the spread of infectious diseases, utilizing technical expertise to maintain sanitary environments in our workplaces, schools, and hospitals; andWHEREAS, California’s status as a global economic leader depends on the dedication of a workforce largely comprised of immigrants who maintain our public and private institutions, and acknowledging the excellence and dedication of California’s janitorial and cleaning workforce serves as a moment to honor these workers not only as cleaners but as vital members of our community fabric; andWHEREAS, The diligent efforts of these frontline professionals ensure that California’s public and private spaces meet the highest standards of hygiene, directly contributing to the physical well-being and safety of all residents; andWHEREAS, The labor of janitors and other property service workers is often performed during late-night or early morning hours, and proclaiming a day to honor the excellence and dedication of California’s janitorial and cleaning workforce provides a necessary opportunity to bring this vital, often “invisible,” work into the light of public appreciation; andWHEREAS, It is fitting and proper to recognize the dedication, resilience, and hard work of the thousands of janitors who keep the Golden State shining and safe; andWHEREAS, Cleanliness is essential to public health, wellness, and prosperity, it enhances productivity, boosts customer satisfaction, preserves vital resources, and supports sustainable, efficient practices; andWHEREAS, According to ISSA, the Association for Cleaning and Facility Solutions, properly cleaned and disinfected surfaces reduce virus concentrations by 41.7 percent, and when paired with effective hand hygiene, that reduction increases to 85.4 percent; andWHEREAS, Cleanliness directly supports a healthy workforce by reducing absenteeism and presenteeism. In 2024 alone, the global economy lost an estimated 438 billion dollars in productivity due to employees unable to perform at full capacity because of illness or injury; andWHEREAS, From manufacturers and distributors producing and delivering essential cleaning and hygiene products to frontline professionals maintaining healthy, safe environments in workplaces, our schools, hospitals, and public spaces, these individuals and businesses play a vital role in protecting our communities every day; andWHEREAS, The citizens of California proudly recognize and celebrate the cleaning and facility solutions industries during this annual observance, highlighting the importance of maintaining clean, safe, and sanitary environments at work, in schools, and at home; now, therefore, be itResolved by the Assembly of the State of California, That the Assembly proclaims April 27, 2026, as a day to honor the excellence and dedication of California’s janitorial and cleaning workforce; and be it furtherResolved, That the Chief Clerk of the Assembly transmit copies of this resolution to the author for appropriate distribution.___________________ REVISIONS:Heading—Line 2.___________________
