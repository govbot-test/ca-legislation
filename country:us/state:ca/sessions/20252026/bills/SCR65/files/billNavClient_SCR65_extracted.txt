[ Filed with Secretary of State July 03, 2025. ] LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTSCR 65, Alvarado-Gil. California Fairgrounds Appreciation Month.This measure would designate the month of May 2025 as California Fairgrounds Appreciation Month in appreciation for the services provided to California by the 77 fairgrounds in the state.Digest Key Fiscal Committee: NO  Bill TextWHEREAS, The history of fairgrounds in California dates back to the mid-19th century, a time when communities were beginning to take shape in the rapidly developing state. These fairs quickly grew in popularity as they provided a platform for farmers, artisans, and inventors to display their innovations; andWHEREAS, The earliest recorded fair in California was held in 1854 in the City of San Francisco, where agricultural exhibitions were organized to showcase local produce and livestock; andWHEREAS, Over time, the tradition of holding fairs spread throughout the state, with each region developing its own unique flavor and focus. These early fairs laid the groundwork for what would become a beloved institution in California’s cultural heritage; andWHEREAS, California’s 77 fairgrounds serve the entire state, from the County of San Diego to the County of Del Norte, and from the County of Monterey to the County of Mariposa; andWHEREAS, California counties enjoy the year-round economic, recreational, cultural, and social impacts of their local fairgrounds; andWHEREAS, Fairgrounds play a crucial role in economic development and business growth by serving as vibrant hubs for community engagement, commerce, and tourism, and are pivotal in nurturing economic vitality and fostering a thriving business ecosystem across the state; and WHEREAS, Fairgrounds host a wide array of events, from county fairs and agricultural expositions to concerts, trade shows, and festivals, attracting millions of visitors annually. This influx of visitors stimulates local economies by driving demand for hospitality services, including hotels, restaurants, and retail establishments; andWHEREAS, Fairgrounds provide businesses with unique opportunities to showcase products, launch new offerings, and engage directly with consumers, fostering brand visibility and market expansion. In addition, fairgrounds often partner with local educational institutions to promote agricultural education and innovation, contributing to workforce development in key sectors; andWHEREAS, Fairgrounds expend and generate hundreds of millions of new dollars for nonprofit organizations, youth organizations, cities and counties, small businesses, and the state’s General Fund; andWHEREAS, Fairgrounds serve as a communal hub where people from all walks of life come together to share experiences and create memories; andWHEREAS, Fairgrounds offer a space for community engagement and interaction, where the bonds of friendship and community are strengthened. From the thrilling rides and games to the educational exhibits and competitions, fairgrounds provide entertainment and learning opportunities for all ages; andWHEREAS, Fairgrounds are a place where tradition and modernity meet, offering a glimpse into both the history and the future of California’s diverse communities; andWHEREAS, California’s fairgrounds provide safe public facilities specifically designed for animals, pets, and livestock during good times and bad; andWHEREAS, One of the primary functions of fairgrounds is to celebrate and preserve California’s rich agricultural heritage and emphasize the significance of agriculture, helping to ensure that this vital industry continues to thrive; andWHEREAS, Fairs offer a platform for programs foundational to the agricultural education of youth, such as Future Farmers of America (FFA) and the National 4-H Council, to educate the public and students by hosting displays of livestock, produce, and horticulture. These events offer insight into sustainable farming practices and the latest advancements in agricultural technology; andWHEREAS, Competitions, such as those for best livestock or produce, encourage excellence and innovation in the agricultural sector; andWHEREAS, During a state of emergency, a natural disaster, or the ongoing impacts of climate change, California fairgrounds are called into service as medical sites, staging areas for emergency equipment and emergency personnel, and evacuation sites to assist those displaced by a fire or flood; andWHEREAS, The future of fairgrounds lies in their ability to adapt to changing social and economic landscapes while preserving the traditions that make them so beloved. As California continues to grow and evolve, so too must these iconic venues; andWHEREAS, Investments in infrastructure and technology will be crucial to maintaining the relevance and appeal of fairgrounds; andWHEREAS, California’s 77 fairgrounds continue to serve as the recreational, social, and economic hubs of their respective communities; andWHEREAS, By continuing to provide a space for community gathering, economic activity, and cultural celebration, fairgrounds will remain an integral part of California’s identity. They are not just a part of the state’s history but a vibrant and essential element of its future; now, therefore, be itResolved by the Senate of the State of California, the Assembly thereof concurring, That the Legislature hereby designates the month of May 2025 as California Fairgrounds Appreciation Month in appreciation for the services provided to California by the 77 fairgrounds in the state; and be it furtherResolved, That the Legislature extends its warmest regards and appreciation to the thousands of fair volunteers, directors, CEOs, and staff, business supporters, sponsors, and associations who keep the network of California fairgrounds strong, vibrant, relevant, and successful; and be it furtherResolved, That the Secretary of the Senate transmit copies of this resolution to the author for appropriate distribution.
