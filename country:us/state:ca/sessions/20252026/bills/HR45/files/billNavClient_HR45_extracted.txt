LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTHR 45, as introduced, Castillo. Digest KeyBill TextWHEREAS, Animals play a significant role in the lives of Californians, from household pets and service animals to livestock and working animals, and their proper care is a reflection of responsible stewardship and community values; andWHEREAS, Humans have a unique role and responsibility in overseeing the well-being of the animals under their care, ensuring that they are treated humanely and not subjected to unnecessary harm or neglect; andWHEREAS, California law has long recognized that the humane treatment of animals is not only a moral responsibility, but one that promotes public health, safety, and social responsibility; andWHEREAS, Animal Rights Awareness Week promotes education on proper animal care, the importance of preventing cruelty, and the value of responsible ownership, particularly for pets and livestock; andWHEREAS, Local animal shelters, nonprofit organizations, and veterinary professionals provide essential services, including rescue, adoption, treatment, and education, and often operate with limited resources; andWHEREAS, Continued investment in animal welfare infrastructure, including appropriate funding for shelters, local enforcement of anticruelty laws, and public education about the importance of spaying and neutering pets, will ensure that animals are properly cared for in every community across the state; andWHEREAS, A culture of stewardship encourages respect for life, accountability among animal owners, and a shared commitment to ethical standards that serve the interests of both people and animals; andWHEREAS, In accordance with the nationally recognized observance of Animal Rights Awareness Week during the third week of June, it is fitting for California to join in raising awareness of the responsibilities we hold toward the animals entrusted to our care; now, therefore, be itResolved by the Assembly of the State of California, That the Assembly hereby proclaims the week of June 15, 2025, as Animal Rights Awareness Week in the State of California; and be it furtherResolved, That the Assembly encourages Californians to engage in responsible pet and livestock care, support local efforts that promote humane treatment, and advocate for balanced public policies that strengthen both animal welfare and community well-being; and be it furtherResolved, That the Chief Clerk of the Assembly transmit copies of this resolution to animal welfare organizations and local governments as an expression of the Assembly’s support for responsible animal stewardship across the state.___________________ REVISIONS: Heading—Line 2.___________________
